### ❌ R2 — De-optimize `/about` metadata to fix branded cannibalization `[DOWNGRADE → NEEDS-MORE-DATA]`
- **Numbers are real:** "nopixel hacks" → `/` (43 clk/79 impr/54.4%/pos 1.0) vs → `/about` (2 clk/159 impr/**1.26%**/pos 3.3); `/about` also takes "nopixel 4.0 hacks" (140 impr), "nopixel minigames" (78 impr), "nopixel hack" (58 impr). The cannibalization *signature* (same query, two pages alternating, the weak one hogging impressions) is plausibly present.
- **Why the proposed fix fails the debate:**
  1. **The lever is mismatched.** I read the live files: the homepage has **no `metadata` export** (it inherits the layout default `"NoPixel 4.0 Minigames & Hacks - Free Practice Trainer"`), and `/about`'s title is `"About Us - NoPixel 4.0 Minigames Trainer"` — **it doesn't even contain the word "hacks."** Google is ranking `/about` for "nopixel hacks" off **body content**, not the title. Rewriting the `/about` *title/description* therefore won't reliably suppress those impressions — the draft is aiming at the wrong surface.
  2. **Capture isn't guaranteed.** Suppressing `/about` does not guarantee `/` inherits the 159 impressions; Google may simply show the result less.
  3. **Downside:** `/about` currently earns 42 clicks; over-suppression risks losing them for an uncertain homepage gain.
  4. The draft **itself** says "verify in GSC that `/about` and `/` trade impressions on the *same* SERPs before acting" — that verification has not been done.
- **Verdict:** **NEEDS-MORE-DATA.** Keep the *observation*, drop the *metadata action*. If verified as true same-SERP cannibalization, the correct fix is **body de-optimization + an internal link `/about → /`** (out of my surface), not a title swap. **No edit.**
